About EthosCheck

EthosCheck is an open accountability platform. We gather publicly available information about the ethical records of companies, products, brands, and governments — and present it clearly, without scores, rankings, or judgement.

We believe consumers and citizens deserve to see what has been reported. We don't tell you what to think — we show you what's out there, and link to the original source every time.

Track record only — no promises

EthosCheck does not report what companies say they will do, plan to do, or have committed to doing. We only report what has already happened or is currently happening. Press releases, sustainability targets, net zero pledges, and future goals are not findings — they are PR. We ignore them entirely.

What we cover

  • Environmental impact — what has actually happened, not stated targets
  • Labour rights, wages, and working conditions
  • Tax practices and corporate structure
  • Animal welfare
  • Political lobbying and donations
  • Supply chain transparency
  • Parent companies, subsidiaries, and shareholders
  • Government corruption, planning, and public accountability
  • State agency conduct and scandals

Why no scores?

A score implies a definitive judgement. What matters to one person (animal welfare) may matter less to another (tax avoidance). We show you the findings and let you decide — a single number hides too much nuance.

Where does the data come from?

All findings are sourced from credible public sources — NGO reports, investigative journalism, official government records, tribunal reports, court judgements, and academic research. Every finding links to its original source.

Ireland focus

EthosCheck is built in Ireland and prioritises Irish relevance — covering Irish businesses, Irish operations of multinationals, and Irish government accountability. As we grow, we plan to tailor results by the user's country.

How to contribute

Anyone can submit a finding using the form below. We review all submissions before publishing. Findings must include a credible public source — we do not publish unverified claims.
